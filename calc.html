What is Calculus?

Calculus is the branch of mathematics that deals with rates of change and accumulation. It consists of two main branches: differential calculus (dealing with derivatives and rates of change) and integral calculus (dealing with integrals and areas under curves). Together, they form a powerful mathematical framework for understanding continuous change.

Key Concepts

Limits

The foundation of calculus. Limits describe what happens to a function as the input approaches a specific value, enabling us to handle infinity and discontinuities.

Continuity

A function is continuous if you can draw it without lifting your pen. Continuity is essential for applying calculus theorems and techniques.

Fundamental Theorem

The bridge connecting derivatives and integrals. It states that differentiation and integration are inverse operations of each other.
