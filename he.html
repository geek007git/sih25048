What is Heredity?

Heredity is the passing of traits from parents to offspring through genetic information. DNA carries the instructions for all inherited characteristics, from eye color to disease susceptibility. This genetic inheritance is the foundation for evolution and the diversity of life.

DNA Structure Visualization

Core Concepts

H
Heredity & Genetics

The study of how traits are passed from parents to offspring through genes. Includes dominant and recessive alleles, chromosomes, and inheritance patterns discovered by Gregor Mendel.

Key Topics: DNA, genes, alleles, chromosomes, Mendelian inheritance

E
Evolution & Natural Selection

The process by which species change over time through natural selection, genetic drift, and other mechanisms. Explains the diversity of life and common ancestry of all organisms.

Key Topics: Natural selection, adaptation, speciation, fossil evidence

Mendelian Inheritance

Gregor Mendel discovered the basic principles of inheritance through his pea plant experiments. His laws explain how traits are passed from generation to generation through dominant and recessive alleles.

Natural Selection in Action

Natural selection is the process by which organisms with favorable traits survive and reproduce more successfully than those without these traits. This leads to evolutionary change over time.

Lesson Summary

What you've learned:

  • Heredity is the passing of traits from parents to offspring through DNA
  • Mendel discovered the basic laws of inheritance using pea plants
  • Dominant and recessive alleles determine which traits are expressed
  • Evolution occurs through natural selection and other mechanisms
  • Natural selection favors traits that improve survival and reproduction
  • Evidence for evolution comes from fossils, DNA, and observed changes
  • Understanding heredity and evolution has important applications in medicine, agriculture, and conservation
